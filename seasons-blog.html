The Growing Season
Something has changed in this part. I lost my sense of freedom to experiment. It has to do with the fact that I am very alive to what happens around me. It is the offset of being observant and open to your surroundings, which does support my work. I suppose it makes me feel the world around me very profoundly, the way I experience color, taste and structure. It often leads to in-depth conversations and allows me to approach things from an unexpected angle. The offset then is that I find it hard to distinct whether a feeling belongs to me or to my surroundings. 

Trinca has just started rooting. There’s the shaping of the team, the creation of the dishes and the menu, the integration of a work structure and then finding a routine. And in the end finding a balance in that which what you put out there also comes back to you. It has been fascinating to witness some days of their growing and inspired way of working. At the same time, unconsciously, I forgot to separate myself. With every stroke, I was no longer playing but feeling I needed to produce to be able to post it online and attract people to come have a look, and especially, eat. I felt pressured.

I came to realize that for me a month is too short to both ‘seed’ and ‘harvest’. Yes, the time pressure makes me produce and work almost every day. Which I love, which is a really good thing. But it just is not possible to create the same quality work as the ones that I exhibited from the first day. In hindsight a ‘Seeding’ month now and a ‘Harvesting’ somewhere in spring would have worked because the ‘Growing’ in my case, has to be in my studio. With time and attention and certainly out of the ‘pleasing’ mode. Still a lot to learn but feeling more conscious now. 

The theme Roots and roots is very inspiring. It will keep me going also after the collaboration. I realize it has been a theme long before, looking at some earlier work. Incorporating the “Dia de los Muertos” made a lot of sense but but the time-span is too short to work it out the way it deserves, there is so much to it. I feel I want to explore much longer and in depth. 
​
I am grateful to Trinca for giving me the opportunity to learn so many things in such a short timespan and for being a wonderful source of inspiration and I hope the presence of myself and my works have provided the team and the clients with joy. 

​


The Seeding season
This first part of this collaboration has been an adventure and I realize how courageous Trinca has been to invite me into this when they have only started rooting themselves two months ago. While many people dream of having their own bar or restaurant only few realize how much it really takes. 

It is inspiring to feel how we all share our joy in the beauty of ingredients, the importance of knowing their origin, both literally and culturally and our belief that creativity is crucial whether we talk restaurant or studio.
I was so pleasantly surprised that the team wants to work with Fruta Feia! Much more interesting than the boring regular facial features of supermarket veggies... 

It is scary to be confronted with my fears. Fear to show my work, fear for it not to be appreciated, fear to set my (time)limits, fear to not be able to produce at a life painting session and yes, fear for the social media circus in general. 

Many seeds have set, back and forth. Stories and backgrounds of the Trincazinhos made my mind run wild with possibilities, as have kitchen utensils, mean machines, fruit arrangements, guests and the stimulation interior. My cookbooks and tales of  the food I remember from childhood left a lovely trace in the kitchen.

The word 'flow' has often crossed my mind. This state of mind in which we are most creative, slightly out of our comfort zone, challenged but still feeling safe. Looking back I feel that Trinca & Neeltje have completed part 1, Seeding, by defining this kind of context for the part to come, Growing.

Although I dare say we already did some serious growing, also on a personal level and together we are discovering what is needed to reach full bloom.
